Uganda is a landlocked country in East Africa, renowned for its diverse ecosystems and rich natural heritage. It is home to over 1,000 species of plants and animals, many of which are endemic to the region. Uganda is best known for its mountain gorillas, which inhabit the Bwindi Impenetrable Forest, as well as its stunning landscapes, including Lake Victoria, the source of the Nile River, and the Rwenzori Mountains. The country also boasts a rich cultural heritage, with over 50 different ethnic groups contributing to its vibrant traditions and history.
